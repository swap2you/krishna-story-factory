Story 006 — The Birth of Lord Krishna

Scriptural Source

Krishna Book Chapter 3 Complete Krishna Book Chapter 3

Recap

Last time in our Krishna Book Bedtime series, we listened to how the heavenly demigods, like Lord Brahmā and Lord Śiva, gathered in celestial gardens to offer their prayers. Even in the darkness of the royal prison, Devakī felt a sacred peace as Lord Krishna entered her womb. The whole universe waited quietly, sensing that a great and wonderful miracle was about to happen on earth, though only those with special hearts could feel it. Now we move to the very special night when Lord Krishna finally appears in this world.

Main Story

In the ancient kingdom of Mathurā, dark clouds gathered over the city, swirling across the midnight sky. Rain started to fall, dancing on the rooftops and filling the palace gardens with cool, fresh scents. Even the animals grew quiet, as if listening for something magical.

Inside the cold, stone prison, Devakī and Vasudeva sat close together on the hard floor. Heavy chains bound their ankles and wrists, and thick iron bars blocked every window. But within Devakī’s heart, a gentle peace blossomed, shining brighter than any jewel.

That night, their hope was like a hidden flame in the darkness.

Midnight approached. Suddenly, a mystical calm swept through Mathurā. The guards on duty in the prison began to feel strangely drowsy.

Swords clattered softly to the floor as heads drooped, and one by one, the guards—all famous for their alertness—fell into an enchanted sleep. Even the locked and barred doors of the prison began to shimmer with an otherworldly light.

Devakī closed her eyes. She felt a warmth bloom inside her, like a gentle sun rising. Light filled the prison cell, growing brighter and brighter until it sparkled against the stone walls.

Suddenly, Lord Krishna appeared, not as a tiny infant, but in His awe-inspiring four-armed form.

His skin was deep blue, like a rain-filled cloud before a storm. In His hands, He held a conch shell, a shining discus, a heavy club, and a pretty pink lotus. His golden crown and glittering jewels seemed to make even the torchlight dim.

Lord Krishna’s eyes radiated compassion and wisdom, and His presence made Devakī and Vasudeva forget all their troubles.

With tears in their eyes, Vasudeva and Devakī offered respectful obeisances and prayers, their hearts bursting with gratitude. Vasudeva softly spoke, "O Supreme Lord, You have kindly appeared before us. Only You can rescue all living beings from fear and sadness."

Lord Krishna smiled peacefully. His voice echoed sweetly in the air, calming every fear. He explained that He would now take the form of a little child, so the world could witness His loving nature.

He told His parents not to be afraid—He would always protect them, and soon they would understand more about His secret plans.

As Lord Krishna finished speaking, His beautiful four-armed form slowly faded. In its place, in a swirl of gentle, golden light, appeared a perfectly formed baby boy. His dark, curly hair framed His glowing face, and His lotus-like eyes looked up at His parents with love.

A soft yellow cloth wrapped His tiny body, and even as a newborn, a trace of the Kaustubha jewel sparkled on His graceful chest.

Every corner of the prison became filled with light and peace. Even the cold stones seemed warm as little Krishna let out His first delicate cries. Vasudeva felt tears of joy run down his cheeks, while Devakī held her precious child closely, losing all her fears in the sweetness of His presence.

At that moment, the city of Mathurā transformed. The river Yamunā, normally wild and powerful, became calm and smooth as silk, its waters glowing with moonlight and lotuses. Midnight flowers blossomed across gardens and roadsides, birds and peacocks began to sing and dance, and gentle breezes swept away bad memories.

The hearts of the kind and the pure grew lighter, while the hearts of the cruel felt uneasy.

But Lord Krishna’s arrival was only the beginning. As if guided by an invisible hand, Vasudeva felt a gentle urge to act. The heavy iron chains binding his hands and feet fell away instantly, clattering to the floor without a single wound.

The solid prison doors, once stubborn and unmovable, unlocked and swung wide open without a sound, welcoming him to step outside.

Vasudeva wrapped baby Krishna in a soft cloth, cradled Him in loving arms, and prepared to step into the stormy night. Although he expected the baby to be heavy, Krishna felt as light as a blossom, radiating a wonderful warmth.

As Vasudeva went outside, rain still poured from the sky. Suddenly, a massive, many-hooded serpent—Ananta Śeṣa—emerged, gliding silently beside him. The serpent gently spread his great hoods wide, forming a living umbrella over Vasudeva and Krishna.

Not a single drop touched them. Lifting his head high, Ananta Śeṣa seemed to clear the path, bringing comfort through the thunder and lightning.

When Vasudeva reached the banks of the Yamunā, he saw the waters swirling by. Fearing for Krishna’s safety, he paused—but at that very moment, the river parted, making a clear, glimmering path just for them. Lotus flowers floated to the surface, and the water grew shallow at each step Vasudeva took.

Frogs and little fish watched in amazement, knowing something divine was happening.

Slowly, Vasudeva reached the nearby village of Gokula, home of Nanda Mahārāja and Yaśodā. Inside their peaceful cottage, another baby—Yogamāyā, born to Yaśodā—slept in a small cradle. Following Krishna’s gentle instructions, Vasudeva quietly exchanged the babies, leaving Krishna safely with Yaśodā and bringing Yogamāyā back with him.

The entire journey was guided by Krishna’s unseen power. The moment Vasudeva returned to the prison, everything returned to normal. The doors softly closed by themselves, the chains re-fastened around his ankles, and the guards awoke, unaware anything had changed.

But in their hearts, Devakī and Vasudeva now cherished a joyful secret: Krishna had safely arrived in the world.

Outside, the stars twinkled brighter than ever, and peace floated on the cooling night air. From that night on, the world would be forever blessed by Krishna’s loving pastimes and gentle presence.

Devotional Meaning

The birth of Lord Krishna is filled with hope and wonder. It shows us that even in the darkest, most difficult places, Krishna’s love finds a way to shine. Devakī and Vasudeva had deep faith, and because of this, Krishna personally came to them and protected everyone from harm. By remembering Krishna’s appearance in the prison—transforming that gloomy place into a home for His pastimes—we learn how the Lord is always with His devotees, offering care, wisdom, and light when it’s needed most. This pastime encourages us to trust Krishna, even when things seem impossible.

Five Lessons

  1. The birth of Lord Krishna is filled with hope and wonder. It shows us that even in the darkest, most difficult places, Krishna’s love finds a way to shine. Devakī and Vasudeva had deep faith, and because of this, Krishna personally came to them and protected everyone from harm. By remembering Krishna’s appearance in the prison—transforming that gloomy place into a home for His pastimes—we learn how the Lord is always with His devotees, offering care, wisdom, and light when it’s needed most. This pastime encourages us to trust Krishna, even when things seem impossible.
  2. Divine events always bring peace and happiness to the world.
  3. Trust Lord Kṛṣṇa even when the path feels uncertain.
  4. Speak truthfully and keep your promises with love.
  5. Remember the Lord with a soft and grateful heart.

Think About It

  1. Who were Krishna’s parents and where were they when He was born?
  2. What special form did Lord Krishna first show before becoming a baby?
  3. How did Vasudeva carry Krishna safely out of the prison?
  4. Why do you think divine miracles happened in Mathurā on the night Krishna was born?
  5. How does this story help you feel brave when facing difficulties?

Five-Star Challenge

  1. Draw a picture of Lord Krishna as a newborn baby in the prison.
  2. Act out the scene where Vasudeva carries Krishna across the river (use a blanket and toy!).
  3. Share tonight’s story with your family or friends, using your own words.
  4. Learn and chant the Hare Krishna mantra three times before bed.
  5. Write a thank-you note to Krishna for His kindness in your life.

Parent/Teacher Note

This episode draws from Krishna Book Chapter 3, offering a child-friendly retelling of Lord Krishna’s divine birth in Mathurā’s prison. The story follows the source closely, gently emphasizing Krishna’s protection, parental love, and the miraculous events described. Invite discussion about how Krishna brings hope even in difficult times and how the mood of devotion is shown by Vasudeva and Devakī. If possible, read together and encourage reflection on how the Lord’s protection appears in daily life.
